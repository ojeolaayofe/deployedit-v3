What is Wireless Network?

A wireless network that enables devices to stay connected to one network while roaming untethered to wires. Being far from a router, devices remain connected to the network with the help of WiFi.



Here, it is important to note that this wireless network lifecycle consists of the following components. This lifecycle includes:



“Research, Design, Install, Asses and Optimize – Wireless Network Lifecycle”

At Deployed IT Solutions, our well-reputed wireless network lifecycle has been useful on many projects.



1. Combine Research

It is key to note that a successful wireless network project is laid with critically planning each stage. For well-performing wireless networks, it is essential to gather the information that will impact the design.

The first stage is a ‘requirement finding stage’ whereby stakeholders point out and document the business and its technical prerequisites. In this stage, one needs to set goals after closely understanding the unique requirements a client may have. At Deployed IT Solutions, our experts come up with realistic milestones to set.

2. Design

After carefully understanding what the client wants, in this second stage, one needs to develop creative models for wireless network design. To generate access point positions, this approach utilizes building floor plans and specifications obtained. The precision of the wireless design will be influenced by building materials and the power needed.

In a predictive model, an AP-on-a-Stick survey can improve the precision of a template. Based on the environment, APoS collects on-site measurements of signal intensity and attenuation. It takes more time and money to complete this process, but the result is more desirable due to the wireless network design precision. Wireless network capacity is vital, considering that the coverage is given in the modern wireless networks, and adding more access points does not necessarily increase the capacity.



3. Deploy

This is the stage when you deploy your designed prototype to check out its performance. It involves acquiring the hardware and configuring the decided area. In this step, mounting access points is crucial, including exact orientation and position. For network technicians, antennas may add difficulty but can be effective with thorough documentation, such as the antenna angle and the connectors’ proper use.
For a number of wireless networks, connect to a network switch, it is necessary to have the right amount of control over Ethernet specified, and it must be carefully calculated. Ideally, to take advantage of zero-touch provisioning, proceed towards cloud-managed network switches. A wireless network must satisfy the environment’s requirements while maintaining good aesthetics and not compromising the equipment’s efficiency. At Deployed IT Solutions, our professionals conduct a thorough heat map survey to find the possible areas resulting in weak signals before proceeding with the installation.



4. Validate and Operate

Just as any other project in life, after installing the modeled structure, it is highly important to critically analyze it for optimum operations. A wireless site validation survey will verify that the configuration meets the criteria of the wireless network design. An expert will conduct spectrum analysis during the on-site wireless site validation survey to classify sources of interference or frequencies that might be less suitable.

5. Optimize

Optimization is something that happens after the launch, completing a life cycle of the wireless network deployment. Wireless networks run on an unlicensed frequency which is vulnerable to interferences and other causes of wireless loss.
Due to high consumption or transmit power changes due to an access point going offline, it is advised to consider that wireless network optimization may require channel modifications. Additional configurations are known to provide better operations. At this stage, you must know that additions to the office or inside a facility can need wireless network optimization as it requires tuning. Troubleshooting wireless issues can help assess the optimization bet
